## The Dissolution of Effort

There's a phase in learning any instrument where you're consciously thinking about technique. Where your fingers go. How to read the notation. The mechanics of breath or posture. Everything requires attention.

Then, if you practice enough, something shifts. The technique stops being something you *do* and becomes something you *are*. Concert pianists describe their hands as having their own intelligence. The fingers know things the conscious mind doesn't track anymore.

The knowledge hasn't disappeared. It's just moved somewhere deeper. Become atmospheric. Part of the background against which everything else happens.

Maybe this is the difference between *knowing* and *knowing how to find*. Between understanding and lookup. The jazz pianist doesn't have a mental search function for scales. The scales *are* the function.

## The Atmosphere of Competence

What interests me is the space between these modes. The moment when searched knowledge becomes ambient. When the thing you looked up enough times stops needing to be looked up.

Is that what learning actually is? Not accumulation but dissolution? Not adding knowledge but letting it become invisible infrastructure?
